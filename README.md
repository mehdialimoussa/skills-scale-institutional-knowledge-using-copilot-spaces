# OctoAcme Project Management Processes

This repository centralizes OctoAcme’s project management best practices as living documentation. These guides help teams carry out consistent, high-quality delivery and make project management knowledge accessible to everyone.

## Purpose

- Centralize scattered project management knowledge in Copilot Spaces
- Convert tacit team insights into searchable, versioned artifacts
- Equal access for all team members to processes, decisions, and rationale
- Extract and standardize workflows collaboratively
- Feed improvements into living docs for continuous evolution
- Accelerate onboarding and make project success repeatable

## Process Coverage

All process artifacts are stored in [`docs/`](./) and organized according to the major phases:

### 1. **Project Management Overview**
Defines principles, core roles (PM, Product Manager, Developer, Stakeholder), lifecycle stages, key artifacts (charter, backlog, risk register), and regular communication rhythms.

### 2. **Project Initiation**
Outlines how projects start:
- Business case & objectives
- Stakeholder identification
- Success criteria and one-pager template
- Initiation checklist

### 3. **Project Planning**
Turns approved work into a delivery plan:
- Kickoff meetings, backlog creation, estimation/scoping
- Definition of Done
- Release planning, risk/dependency management

### 4. **Execution & Tracking**
- Team rhythm: standups, syncs, demos
- Pull request and CI workflow
- Testing and quality standards
- Reporting and escalation process
- Execution checklist

### 5. **Risk Management & Communication**
- Risk register lifecycle: identify, assess, mitigate, monitor
- Stakeholder update formats
- Incident and escalation paths

### 6. **Release & Deployment**
- Release types (patch/minor/major)
- Pre-release checks, deployment checklist
- Rollback plan and release note template

### 7. **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement**
- Retrospective format, action item tracking
- Measuring and celebrating improvements

### 8. **Roles & Personas**
Responsibilities and typical communication of Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers.

## Contribution and Customization

To propose process improvements, use the issue templates in [`.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/`](../.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/) such as “Add Content to Project Management Process Docs.” All changes are versioned and reviewed before adoption.
